üéØ Null and Alternative Hypotheses

The foundation of statistical hypothesis testing

Definition

Hypothesis Testing is a statistical method used to make decisions about a population parameter based on sample data. It involves comparing observed data against a claim or hypothesis about the population.

H‚ÇÄ

Null Hypothesis (H‚ÇÄ)

The statement of no effect, no difference, or status quo.

  • Always contains an equality (=, ‚â§, or ‚â•)
  • Assumed true until evidence suggests otherwise
  • The hypothesis we are trying to disprove
  • Cannot be "accepted" ‚Äî only fail to reject
H‚ÇÅ

Alternative Hypothesis (H‚ÇÅ or H‚Çê)

The statement of an effect, a difference, or change.

  • Contains inequality (‚â†, <, or >)
  • What we want to prove with evidence
  • The research hypothesis
  • Opposite of the null hypothesis

üåü Rare Events & the p-value

The probability principle behind statistical decisions

The Rare Event Rule

If, under a given assumption (H‚ÇÄ), the probability of a particular observed event is very small, we conclude that the assumption is probably not correct.

üé≤ Understanding the p-value

Definition

p-value = The probability of obtaining a test statistic at least as extreme as the one observed, assuming the null hypothesis is true.


Think of the p-value as answering: "If H‚ÇÄ were true, how surprised should I be by this data?"

  • Small p-value (‚â§ Œ±): The observed result is unlikely if H‚ÇÄ is true ‚Üí Reject H‚ÇÄ
  • Large p-value (> Œ±): The observed result is reasonably likely if H‚ÇÄ is true ‚Üí Fail to reject H‚ÇÄ
